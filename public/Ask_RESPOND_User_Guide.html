Ask RESPOND Brentwood

User Guide for Staff
Brentwood Community College · Skills For Life

Ask RESPOND is a safeguarding thinking partner. It helps you think through what you are seeing, organise your observations, and work out your next step. It does not make safeguarding decisions and it does not replace the DSL or your own professional judgement.

In an emergency, always act first. If someone is in immediate danger, call 999. Do not consult the tool before acting in a life-threatening situation. Use the Emergency Contacts tab in the app for the full list of numbers.

What it is, and what it is not

It can help you

  • Make sense of a behaviour change or a concern
  • Decide how urgent something is
  • Understand the right next step and who to tell
  • Find the correct contact or referral route
  • Check what a policy or framework says

It will not

  • Decide whether something "counts" as abuse
  • Make a referral for you
  • Replace the DSL or a conversation with a colleague
  • Store your chat or keep a formal record
  • Tell you a concern can stay between you and it

Getting started

  1. Open the tool and enter the access code. Your DSL or line manager will give you the current code.
  2. Read and accept the short notice on the login screen.
  3. Type your question or describe what you have seen in plain words. You can also tap the microphone to speak.
  4. Read the response. It will start with a colour-coded priority and then talk you through it.
  5. Act on it. Record on CPOMS and speak to the DSL where needed — the tool will tell you when.
Protect privacy. Never type a student's real name or anything that identifies them. Use initials, "Student A", or a general description. The tool does not save your conversation, but good practice is to keep it anonymous.

Understanding the priority colours

Every response to a concern begins with one tag, so you can see at a glance how urgent it is.

REDImmediate action needed. Possible serious harm or active risk. Contact the DSL now; if out of hours and risk is immediate, call 999.
AMBERSignificant concern. Share with the DSL today.
GREENStandard process. Record on CPOMS and notify the DSL through the normal route.
BLUEInformation or a policy question. No active concern.
GREYGeneral guidance, training or a question about the app.

What you can ask

Talk to it as you would a trusted colleague. For example:

"A student is suddenly distressed during personal care with one staff member. What should I do?" "His self-injurious behaviour has gone from a few times a month to several times a day. Is that a concern?" "A student selected the 'hurt' and 'home' symbols on their AAC. How should I handle this?" "Who do I contact for an 18-year-old student?" "What does the Mental Capacity Act say about best interests?" "Where do I record a restrictive physical intervention?"

The golden rules it will always reinforce

  • Behaviour is communication. A change from a student's baseline can be how they tell you something is wrong. Never dismiss distress as "just their condition".
  • Never promise confidentiality. You cannot keep a safeguarding concern secret, and you must not offer false reassurance.
  • Pause before acting. Do not investigate, do not confront anyone, and do not contact a family member who may be involved. That is the DSL's role.
  • A concern cannot be passed from one staff member to another. If you hold the concern, you record and report it.
  • Always record on CPOMS and notify the DSL where the tool advises it. Notify and Document are required in every case.

Features in the app

  • Microphone — speak your question instead of typing.
  • Speaker — have responses read aloud.
  • Print — produce a tidy record of a conversation if you need one.
  • Emergency Contacts tab — the full contact list, age-based routes included.
  • Accessibility settings — adjust text size and display (press A).
  • Clear chat / quick hide — clear the conversation or hide the screen quickly if someone approaches.

If you are ever unsure

Speak to the DSL or a Deputy DSL. The tool is there to help you think, but a real conversation with the safeguarding team is always the right move when something does not feel right. When in doubt, report it.
